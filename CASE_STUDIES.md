# Case Studies

**Last Updated:** January 2026

---

## 1. Overview

This document presents a series of case studies that demonstrate the application and effectiveness of the Decision Assessment Assistant (DAA) platform in real-world scenarios. Each case study provides a detailed analysis of a complex decision, comparing the DAA's recommendation with the actual outcome.

---

## 2. Case Study 1: "Should I Switch Jobs?"

### 2.1. Scenario

- **Subject:** A mid-career software engineer.
- **Decision:** Choose between staying at a large, stable corporation (Job A) or joining a fast-growing startup (Job B).
- **Data:** The subject provided detailed information about both offers, including salary, benefits, job responsibilities, and personal priorities.

### 2.2. DAA Analysis

- **Pack Used:** `switch-jobs-pack`
- **Key Criteria & Weights:**
  - Financial Impact (35%)
  - Career Growth (30%)
  - Work-Life Balance (20%)
  - Job Security (15%)

| Option | Financial Impact | Career Growth | Work-Life Balance | Job Security | **Total Score** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Job A (Corporation)** | 8.0 | 6.0 | 8.5 | 9.5 | **7.88** |
| **Job B (Startup)** | 9.0 | 9.5 | 6.0 | 6.0 | **8.10** |

- **Risk Analysis:**
  - **Job A:** Low risk (market stability, established company).
  - **Job B:** Medium risk (startup volatility, high pressure).

- **DAA Recommendation:** **ACCEPT Job B** (with a confidence score of 85%). The higher potential for career growth and financial reward outweighed the increased risk.

### 2.3. Actual Outcome

- The subject accepted Job B.
- **After 1 year:** The startup successfully secured a new round of funding, and the subject was promoted to a team lead position. The subject reported high job satisfaction, despite the longer working hours.
- **Conclusion:** The DAA's recommendation was validated by the positive outcome.

## 3. Case Study 2: "Should I Learn This New Technology?"

### 3.1. Scenario

- **Subject:** A freelance web developer.
- **Decision:** Invest time in learning a new JavaScript framework (Framework X) or focus on mastering their existing skillset.
- **Data:** The subject provided information about their career goals, current market trends, and the learning curve of the new framework.

### 3.2. DAA Analysis

- **Pack Used:** `learn-tech-pack`
- **Key Criteria & Weights:**
  - Market Demand (40%)
  - Skill Relevance (30%)
  - Learning Effort (20%)
  - Personal Interest (10%)

| Option | Market Demand | Skill Relevance | Learning Effort | Personal Interest | **Total Score** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Learn Framework X** | 9.0 | 8.5 | 6.0 | 7.0 | **8.05** |
| **Master Existing Skills** | 7.0 | 7.5 | 9.0 | 8.0 | **7.65** |

- **Risk Analysis:**
  - **Learn Framework X:** Medium risk (the framework might not become popular).
  - **Master Existing Skills:** Low risk (stable demand for existing skills).

- **DAA Recommendation:** **LEARN Framework X** (with a confidence score of 80%). The high market demand and relevance to future projects justified the learning effort.

### 2.3. Actual Outcome

- The subject decided to learn Framework X.
- **After 6 months:** The subject landed two high-paying projects that specifically required Framework X. The new skills significantly increased their hourly rate.
- **Conclusion:** The DAA's recommendation proved to be financially and professionally beneficial.

## 4. Case Study 3: "Should I Start a Side Project?"

### 4.1. Scenario

- **Subject:** A marketing manager with an idea for a new mobile app.
- **Decision:** Invest personal time and money to develop the app or focus on their primary career.
- **Data:** The subject provided a business plan, market research, and a personal financial assessment.

### 4.2. DAA Analysis

- **Pack Used:** `side-project-pack`
- **Key Criteria & Weights:**
  - Market Potential (35%)
  - Personal Fit (25%)
  - Financial Viability (20%)
  - Execution Risk (20%)

| Option | Market Potential | Personal Fit | Financial Viability | Execution Risk | **Total Score** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Start Side Project** | 8.0 | 9.0 | 6.0 | 5.0 | **7.25** |
| **Focus on Career** | 6.0 | 7.0 | 9.0 | 9.0 | **7.45** |

- **Risk Analysis:**
  - **Start Side Project:** High risk (market competition, technical challenges, financial investment).
  - **Focus on Career:** Low risk (stable income, clear career path).

- **DAA Recommendation:** **CAUTION** on starting the side project (with a confidence score of 65%). The high execution risk and uncertain financial viability were significant concerns.

### 2.3. Actual Outcome

- The subject decided to start the side project despite the DAA's cautious recommendation.
- **After 1 year:** The project failed to gain traction and was eventually abandoned. The subject reported financial losses and burnout.
- **Conclusion:** The DAA's risk assessment was accurate, and its cautious recommendation was justified.
